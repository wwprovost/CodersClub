The modulus operator is also the gateway to finding a whole-number quotient when a number is not evenly divisible by another. The trick is to take the modulus as the remainder, subtract it, and then divide the remaining value, which now must be a multiple of the divisor.

The modulus operator is also pairs up nicely with a whole-number quotient. Remember that, in Python, the / operator gives you the whole-number quotient, and loses the remainder.

// As a decimal: number result = 35 / 8; print line "35 divided by 8 is " + result; // As quotient and remainder, or mixed number: number remainder = 35 % 8; number evenMultiple = 35 - remainder; number quotient = evenMultiple / 8; print line "... or " + quotient + " and " + remainder + " eighths.";
# As a decimal: print "35 divided by 8 is", 35.0 / 8.0 # As quotient and remainder, or mixed number: remainder = 35 % 8 quotient = 35 / 8 print "... or", quotient, "and", remainder, " eighths."
